- [Lindsay Tedds on the long-form census](https://deadfortaxreasons.wordpress.com/2026/05/05/long-form-long-memory/): “You should fill it out because the census is the closest thing this country has to a mirror. Demographic, social, economic — every five years Canada looks at itself and writes down what it sees. And in 2026, 1 in 4 of you will get the long form, the version that asks the questions that actually shape policy.”
- [Alan Jacobs on the Canvas hack](https://blog.ayjay.org/predictable/) (on which, see [CBC](https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/canvas-cyber-attack-canadian-universities-9.7193648) or [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Canvas_security_incident)): “Every university function that is on the internet is a security vulnerability. (Just [look](https://systemstatus.baylor.edu/) at how many online systems we have!) But every university function outsourced to a giant company whose tools are used by many universities is a far greater vulnerability, because there is so much money to be made from exploiting all that data. Locally owned and managed data is a smaller and less appealing target for hackers.”
- [Good Sudoku](https://www.playgoodsudoku.com/) is a brilliant Sudoku implementation. Just the right level of assistance, with interaction design that makes it a delight to play. ([via Dave Rupert](https://daverupert.com/2026/05/vibe-check-42/))

I clearly remember playing Sudoku on a 2006 family trip to Italy. Blows my mind that the game’s popularity in the English-speaking world would’ve been so fresh at that point—but that also makes perfect sense, as my playing it would’ve been following that trend.
