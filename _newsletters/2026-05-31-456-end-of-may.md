Hello! Straight into links today, as my brain / body don’t seem to be rowing in sync:

- Buried in the good-news story that [OC Transpo is returning to double-car service](https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/ottawa/double-car-train-service-returning-to-line-1-on-june-8-says-oc-transpo-9.7214291) on the LRT is the nugget that the LRT will return to 5 minute frequency (from 3 to 4 minutes during the single-car service). Enough serviceable trains for double-car service is good, but reduced frequency sucks. Crowded cars are no fun, sure, but more frequent service helped immensely to make the service _feel_ reliable. When you can just show up and shortly catch a train, there’s less rushing, and fewer missed connections. It makes transit feel imaginable. Look, I’m glad to hear that the new OC Transpo GM [takes transit](https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/ottawa/i-came-in-at-the-right-time-new-oc-transpo-chief-says-he-feels-set-up-for-success-9.7201745) (shouldn’t be a news story, but alas), but I wonder how much more often he’ll miss his connection to St-Laurent while riding in slightly less crowded cars.
- Straight out of Kentucky, Troublesome Creek Stringed Instrument Co. is making all sorts of stringed instruments, [introducing woodworking to folks in recovery](https://blog.lostartpress.com/2026/05/26/social-impact-luthiery/). 
- [Cool video of interior-glazed butter bell pottery](https://www.miriamsuzanne.com/2026/05/31/_micro/), where the lid and jar are fired together, then coaxed apart with a mallet!? (From Miriam Suzanne, whose [site colophon is delightful](https://www.miriamsuzanne.com/colophon/).)
